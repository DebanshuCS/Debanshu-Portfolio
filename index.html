About

I am a Computer Science researcher and engineer focused on applied machine learning and production-aware model development. My background includes building specialized systems for NLP and computer vision, such as PALM (a hybrid threat-detection framework utilizing GNNs).

Current Focus: I am addressing a critical gap in healthcare diagnostics: the Early Detection of Knee Osteoarthritis (OA). Because plain radiographs typically fail to detect early-stage OA, I am developing a deep learning architecture that fuses high-resolution MRI scans with clinical patient history. This multimodal approach aims to capture early cartilage loss that X-rays miss. I am currently validating this model on clinical data alongside hospital collaborators.

Research Statement

My research journey began exploring deep learning for early rumor detection on social media. This sparked my curiosity about applying ML to domains with high societal impact, particularly the security of Cyber-Physical Systems (CPS) and, more recently, Medical Imaging.

Current Focus: Medical Imaging

I am currently developing multimodal Deep Learning architectures to detect Knee Osteoarthritis using MRI scans and clinical data, aiming to identify disease markers before they appear on standard X-rays.

Previous Contributions (CPS Security):
In 2023, as a Research Fellow at BITS Pilani, I focused on modeling attacker behaviors. I developed tools that automated log parsing and integrated Petri Nets to simulate adversarial activity. I further explored predictive threat modeling by combining attack graphs with GCNs, culminating in an accepted paper at IEEE PRDC 2024.

Future Directions

I aim to design intelligent systems capable of:

  • Multimodal Fusion: Combining disparate data sources (Imaging + Text + Tabular) for higher diagnostic accuracy.
  • Adaptive Defense: Using GNNs for attack path prediction in critical infrastructure.
  • Scalability: Building NLP-GNN frameworks that operate in real-time environments.
