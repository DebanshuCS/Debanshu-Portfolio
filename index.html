Research Statement

My research journey began with a realization during my undergraduate thesis on early rumor detection: high-accuracy deep learning models are often fragile and opaque. This exposure to the risks of 'black-box' AI shifted my focus toward building systems that are not just predictive, but inspectable. During my fellowship at BITS Pilani & IIT-K, I applied this philosophy to Critical Infrastructure security (Project PAST), utilizing Graph Convolutional Networks (GCNs) to uncover unseen attack paths. I realized a striking parallel exists in Medical Imaging: just like in cybersecurity, radiologists hesitate to trust opaque models. This symmetry drives my current work—designing multimodal architectures that unify neural perception with causal reasoning to detect and explain anomalies in both cyber-physical systems and medical imaging.

Current Focus: Medical Imaging

As a PhD student in the BITS–RMIT joint program, my current research focuses on developing multimodal deep learning models for medical image analysis, with an emphasis on early detection of Knee Osteoarthritis using MRI scans combined with clinical metadata. My objective is to design models that capture subtle structural and contextual biomarkers before they manifest in conventional diagnostic imaging, while maintaining interpretability and clinical relevance.

Future Directions

Moving forward, my research aims to advance robust and explainable AI frameworks that operate reliably in complex, real-world environments. In particular, I am interested in:

  • Multimodal Fusion: Integrating imaging, textual, and structured clinical data to improve diagnostic accuracy and early disease detection.
  • Graph-based Reasoning: Leveraging GNNs to model relational dependencies in biological structures.
  • Explainability & Reliability: Designing interpretable deep learning systems that support human decision-making in critical domain.
