Research Statement

My research journey began during my undergraduate thesis, where I explored the use of deep learning for early rumor detection on social media. This work exposed me to the potential of AI in addressing real-world challenges and sparked my curiosity about applying machine learning to domains with high societal impact, particularly the security of Cyber-Physical Systems (CPS).

In 2023, I advanced this interest as a Research Fellow at BITS Pilani, where I focused on modeling attacker behaviors in CPS. I developed tools that automated log parsing and integrated Petri Nets with perturbation techniques to simulate adversarial activity, work that culminated in a peer-reviewed publication at ICCSN. Building on this foundation, I explored predictive threat modeling by combining attack graphs with graph convolutional networks (GCNs), enabling more accurate attack path prediction. This research was accepted at IEEE PRDC 2024 and demonstrated how integrating threat intelligence with AI can enhance system-level security analysis.

Looking ahead, my research aims to design intelligent, adaptive defense mechanisms for CPS. Specifically, I am investigating:

  • Transformer-based models for semantic log analysis and anomaly detection.
  • Graph Neural Networks for attack path prediction and vulnerability assessment.
  • Scalable NLP-GNN frameworks for CPS security orchestration with reinforcement learning.

By uniting these approaches into robust frameworks, I hope to create real-time orchestration systems capable of detecting and mitigating complex multi-step intrusions such as Advanced Persistent Threats (APTs). Ultimately, my goal is to contribute to the development of AI-driven security frameworks that can adapt to evolving attack strategies, ensuring the resilience of CPS infrastructures. I am eager to continue this work through collaborative, interdisciplinary research that combines rigorous technical methods with a focus on practical impact.
